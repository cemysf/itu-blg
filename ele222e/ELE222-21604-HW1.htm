1.      Define electronvolt (eV) (1 sentence), and give its value in terms of [J].

Electronvolt is the energy acquired by one electron when passing freely through a potential difference of 1 V. 1 eV = 1,602 10-19 J

2.      What are Kirchhoff’s Laws (2 sentences)?

KCL = Kirchhoff’s Current Law: At any point in an electrical circuit, the algebraic sum of all the currents meeting at that point is ZERO.

KVL = Kirchhoff’s Voltage Law: In any closed electrical circuit, the algebraic sum of all the voltages in each part of that closed circuit is ZERO.

3.      What is Ohm’s Law (1 sentence and 1 formula with explanation)?

The electrical current flowing in a resistor is linearly proportional to the applied potential difference across the resistor, i.e., V = R I.

4.      What is a capacitor? (1 sentence) How does capacitance relate to the physical properties of the capacitor? (1 formula with explanation)

A capacitor is a component, an arrangement of two conductors of area A separated by a dielectric material (εo εr!) of thickness d. Capacitance is the property of the capacitor to store electrical charge. C = εo εr A/d.
